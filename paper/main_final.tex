% ABSTRACT
% ============================================================================
\begin{abstract}
Chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning is widely believed to improve LLM accuracy by allowing models to ``think longer.''
We identify a fundamental inefficiency we call the \couplingtax{}: when reasoning traces and final answers share a single output-token budget, they compete for capacity, and truncation---not reasoning quality---becomes the dominant failure mode.
The tax requires two conditions: (i)~the reasoning chain typically exceeds the budget, causing truncation waste, and (ii)~the model possesses a distinct non-thinking mode that can answer without generating a reasoning chain.
Through systematic experiments on GSM8K ($n{=}1{,}319$), MATH-500 ($n{=}500$), and five BIG-Bench Hard tasks ($n{=}1{,}187$) with Qwen3 models at three scales (8B, 9B, 27B) under fixed output-token budgets, we show that non-thinking mode outperforms thinking mode on GSM8K and MATH-500 at every budget $\leq$2048, with gaps exceeding 40 percentage points on harder math benchmarks (BBH reverses at $b{=}2048$, consistent with the crossover shifting as chain lengths shrink).
We develop a truncation-waste decomposition---$\mathrm{Acc}_{\mathrm{think}}(b) = \alpha_c \cdot F_L(b) + \alpha_t \cdot (1-F_L(b))$---that predicts the crossover budget from chain-length statistics alone and explains the inverse scaling with model size within the Qwen family ($2.1\times$ amplification from 8B to 9B/27B, associated with longer reasoning chains).
Cross-architecture experiments on DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B confirm that truncation waste is universal, but the end-to-end tax depends on availability of an efficient alternative mode---DeepSeek's prompt-based nothink produces near-zero tax because it does not change the model's generation behavior.
As a proof-of-concept mitigation, we test \splitbudget{}---allocating separate budgets for reasoning and answering.
Full-scale evaluation ($n{=}500$, same hardware as baselines) confirms gains: split-budget generation at $B_{\text{think}}{=}4096$ achieves 74.0\% [70.0, 77.7]---exceeding nothink@1024 (59.8\%) by +14.2\,pp.
We scope these findings to models with architecturally distinct thinking and non-thinking modes under fixed output-token budgets on structured reasoning tasks.
\end{abstract}
